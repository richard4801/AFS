2. Background

AFS is a fiction publishing studio that sources, edits, and monetises serialised novels on third-party platforms, including but not limited to Kindle Vella, Dreame, Goodnovel, Fizzo, Innovel, and other international publishing platforms (collectively, the “Platforms”). AFS wishes to engage the Writer as an independent contractor to produce original written works on its behalf, and the Writer wishes to accept such engagement, on the terms set out in this Agreement.

3. Nature of Relationship

3.1 Independent Contractor. The Writer is engaged as an independent contractor and not as an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of AFS. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment relationship.

3.2 No Benefits. As an independent contractor, the Writer is not entitled to any employment benefits, including but not limited to health insurance, paid leave, pension contributions, or any statutory employment entitlements.

3.3 Tax Responsibility. The Writer is solely responsible for all applicable taxes, social security contributions, and other statutory deductions arising from payments received under this Agreement in their country of residence. AFS shall have no obligation to withhold taxes on behalf of the Writer unless required to do so by applicable law.

3.4 Other Engagements. The Writer may engage in other professional activities that do not conflict with or breach this Agreement, provided that such activities do not involve creating works that directly compete with the Works (as defined below) on the same Platforms.

4. Work & Deliverables

4.1 Scope of Work. The Writer agrees to produce original serialised fiction (“the Work” or “Works”) under the direction of AFS. The genre, storyline, characters, and other creative elements shall be assigned or approved by AFS. The Writer shall produce a minimum of three (3) chapters per active writing day, each chapter containing no fewer than one thousand (1,000) words unless otherwise specified in writing by AFS.

4.2 Chapter Submissions. All chapters must be submitted via the AFS dashboard for editorial review. AFS reserves the right to request revisions, reject chapters that do not meet editorial or quality standards, and require rewrites before publication. The Writer agrees to complete reasonable revision requests within 48 hours of receipt.

4.3 Quality Standards. All Work must be original, coherent, engaging, and free of plagiarism, excessive AI-generated content, or content that violates the policies of the Platforms. The Writer must maintain consistent tone, character voice, and narrative continuity throughout each Work. AFS may conduct spot-checks for originality using standard detection tools.

4.4 Update Day Requirement. The Writer must maintain a minimum of twenty-five (25) active update days per calendar month. An “active update day” is defined as any calendar day on which the Writer submits and receives approval for a minimum of three (3) chapters. Failure to meet 25 active update days in any calendar month will directly reduce the Work’s visibility ranking on the publishing platform, affecting reader reach and the Writer’s earnings. The Writer’s compliance with this requirement is tracked in real time on the private dashboard calendar.

5. Editorial Review

5.1 Review Process. All submitted chapters are subject to editorial review by AFS. AFS may approve, request revisions, or reject chapters at its sole discretion based on quality, consistency, and platform suitability.

5.2 Turnaround. AFS will endeavour to review submitted chapters within five (5) business days. However, review timelines may vary depending on volume and platform requirements, and AFS does not guarantee a fixed turnaround time.

5.3 Publication Decisions. AFS retains final authority over which chapters are published, the timing of publication, the Platforms on which the Work is published, and any decisions to pause, withdraw, or modify the publication of the Work.

6. Compensation

6.1 Revenue Share. In consideration of the Work produced under this Agreement, AFS shall pay the Writer a share of the net royalties (“Net Revenue”) received by AFS from the Platforms in respect of the Writer’s Work, at the rate agreed between the Parties at the time of onboarding and confirmed in writing. “Net Revenue” means gross royalty payments received by AFS from the Platforms, less any applicable platform fees, transaction charges, currency conversion costs, and any taxes withheld by the Platforms.

6.2 Milestone Bonus. Upon the Work reaching a cumulative total of thirty thousand (30,000) approved and published words, the Writer shall be entitled to a one-time milestone bonus of One Hundred and Twenty United States Dollars (USD $120.00), payable within fourteen (14) calendar days of the milestone being verified by AFS.

6.3 Payment Schedule. Net Revenue payments shall be made on a monthly basis, no later than the fifteenth (15th) day of the month following the month in which the revenues were received by AFS from the Platforms. Payment shall be made to the Writer’s designated payment account specified above, via Payoneer, PayPal, wire transfer, or such other method as the Parties may agree in writing.

6.4 Minimum Payout Threshold. Monthly royalty payments are subject to a minimum balance threshold of One Hundred United States Dollars (USD $100.00). Accrued earnings below this threshold will roll forward and be included in the following month’s payment cycle. This threshold does not apply to the Milestone Bonus described in Section 6.2, which is paid independently of the accrued balance.

6.5 Statements. AFS shall provide the Writer with a monthly earnings statement through the AFS dashboard, detailing the revenues received in respect of their Work and the amount payable to the Writer.

7. Intellectual Property

7.1 Assignment. The Writer hereby irrevocably assigns to AFS, with full title guarantee, all intellectual property rights (including copyright and related rights) in and to the Work and all chapters, drafts, and other materials produced under this Agreement, throughout the world, in perpetuity, in all media now known or hereafter invented.

7.2 Moral Rights. To the extent permitted by applicable law, the Writer irrevocably waives all moral rights in relation to the Work, including the right to be identified as the author and the right to object to derogatory treatment of the Work.

7.3 Work Made for Hire. To the extent the Work qualifies as a “work made for hire” under applicable law, it is intended to be a work made for hire for AFS, and all rights therein shall vest exclusively in AFS.

7.4 No Competing Use. The Writer shall not publish, distribute, or otherwise exploit the Work or any substantial portion thereof independently or through any third party without the prior written consent of AFS, for the duration of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years following its termination.

8. Confidentiality

8.1 Obligation. The Writer acknowledges that in the course of this engagement they may receive or have access to confidential information of AFS, including but not limited to: editorial strategies, platform relationships and rates, unpublished Works, writer identities, business plans, and financial information (“Confidential Information”). The Writer undertakes to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not to disclose it to any third party, during the term of this Agreement and indefinitely thereafter.

8.2 Pen Names. The Writer agrees to publish exclusively under pen names assigned or approved by AFS, and shall not publicly identify themselves as the author of any Work, nor identify AFS as the publishing entity behind any pen name, without the express written consent of AFS.

8.3 Platform Identity. The Writer shall not create or operate accounts on any Platform under the same pen name or any variation of it, independently of AFS, nor shall they contact any Platform directly regarding the Works without the prior written approval of AFS.

9. Plagiarism & AI-Generated Content

9.1 Original Work Only. All Work submitted under this Agreement must be entirely the original creative output of the Writer. Submission of plagiarised content — defined as copying or substantially reproducing existing published or unpublished works without authorisation — is a material breach of this Agreement and may result in immediate termination and a clawback of any payments made in respect of the infringing Work.

9.2 AI-Assisted Content. The use of AI writing tools (including but not limited to ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and similar large-language-model tools) to generate significant portions of submitted Work without disclosure to and approval by AFS is prohibited. The Writer may use AI tools for brainstorming, outlining, or light editing, but all substantive prose must be primarily the Writer’s own creative output. Where AI assistance is used to an extent that materially contributes to the final text, the Writer must disclose this to AFS prior to submission. AFS reserves the right to reject AI-heavy submissions without payment.

10. Term & Termination

10.1 Term. This Agreement commences on {{EFFECTIVE_DATE}} and continues until terminated by either Party in accordance with this Section.

10.2 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate this Agreement by providing fourteen (14) calendar days’ written notice to the other Party. During the notice period, the Writer shall continue to fulfil their obligations under this Agreement.

10.3 Termination for Cause. AFS may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the Writer: (a) commits a material breach of any term of this Agreement (including but not limited to plagiarism, failure to meet quality standards, or breach of confidentiality obligations); (b) becomes insolvent or enters any form of bankruptcy or insolvency proceedings; (c) is convicted of any criminal offence that AFS reasonably considers likely to bring AFS into disrepute; or (d) fails to submit any Work for a continuous period of thirty (30) calendar days without prior written explanation and agreement from AFS.

10.4 Consequences of Termination. Upon termination: (i) the Writer shall immediately cease producing new Work; (ii) AFS shall pay the Writer all Net Revenue accrued up to the effective date of termination (subject to the minimum threshold in Section 6.4, which shall not apply at termination — all accrued net amounts shall be paid in full regardless of balance); (iii) the intellectual property assignment in Section 7 and the confidentiality obligations in Section 8 shall survive termination indefinitely.

12. Dispute Resolution

12.1 Good Faith Negotiation. In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, the Parties shall first attempt to resolve the matter amicably through good faith negotiations. Either Party may initiate this process by giving written notice of the dispute to the other.

12.2 Mediation. If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation within thirty (30) calendar days of the notice referred to in Section 12.1 (or such longer period as the Parties may agree in writing), either Party may refer the matter to mediation, administered by a mutually agreed mediator or, failing agreement, an independent mediator appointed by a recognised mediation body.

12.3 Arbitration. If the dispute is not resolved through mediation within a further thirty (30) calendar days, it shall be finally resolved by binding arbitration. The arbitration shall be conducted by a sole arbitrator agreed upon by the Parties or, failing agreement, appointed by the relevant arbitral institution. The language of arbitration shall be English.

12.4 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales (or such other jurisdiction as the Parties may agree in writing at the time of signing), without regard to its conflict of law provisions.
